## General high-severity
- **PAN-OS GlobalProtect CVE-2026-0257 — CISA KEV deadline today.** Authentication-override-cookie bypass against GlobalProtect portal/gateway; with the right cert config an unauth attacker forges a cookie and lands as the local admin. Palo Alto's advisory was re-updated late on 2026-05-30 and Rapid7's MDR continues to observe successful exploitation across customers since 2026-05-17. Federal civilian remediation due **2026-06-01** per CISA KEV — i.e. the deadline is the news of the day for US federal teams. Expect a flood of post-deadline compromise reports starting late this week. [Palo Alto advisory](https://security.paloaltonetworks.com/CVE-2026-0257), [Rapid7 ETR](https://www.rapid7.com/blog/post/etr-rapid7-observed-exploitation-of-pan-os-globalprotect-authentication-bypass-vulnerability-cve-2026-0257/), [Threat-Modeling.com VIR 2026-05-31](https://threat-modeling.com/vulnerability-intelligence-report-may-31-2026/) — KEV deadline 2026-06-01 — HIGH.
- **WP Maps Pro CVE-2026-8732 — active exploitation reported 2026-05-31.** Hardcoded-admin temporary-access AJAX endpoint (`wpgmp_temp_access_ajax_callback`) creates a `wp_insert_user()` with role=`administrator`, username=random, email=`support@flippercode.com` when called with `check_temp=false` — completely unauth. ~15,800 paid licences (Envato), affects ≤ 6.1.0; patched 2026-05-20 in 6.1.1 with a capability check on the AJAX action. BleepingComputer 2026-05-31 reports in-the-wild exploitation, not just disclosure — Wordfence-tracked indicators include rogue admin users with that exact email. [BleepingComputer](https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/wp-maps-pro-bug-exploited-to-create-admin-accounts-on-wordpress-sites/), [TheCyberExpress](https://thecyberexpress.com/wp-maps-pro-vulnerability/) — 2026-05-31 — HIGH.
- **Apache Airflow — cluster disclosure on oss-security, 2026-05-31.** Multiple advisories posted in one batch covering 3.x. Headline is **CVE-2026-42359 — authenticated RCE via XCom PATCH endpoint**. The set also includes **CVE-2026-49298** (JWT token exposure in KubernetesExecutor command-line args), **CVE-2026-48726** (`revoke_token()` unreachable in FabAuthManager/KeycloakAuthManager logout — token revocation silently no-ops), **CVE-2026-46764** (Event Log detail endpoint bypasses DAG-scoped permission filter — info disclosure across DAGs), **CVE-2026-45426** (log-server JWT authz bypass via Python `lstrip()` character stripping), **CVE-2026-45360** (arbitrary import in custom deadline-reference deserialization). Upgrade path is Airflow 3.2.x. CVSS not centrally published yet across the set; the XCom PATCH RCE is the obvious priority. [oss-security 2026/05](https://www.openwall.com/lists/oss-security/2026/05/), [Apache Airflow security](https://airflow.apache.org/docs/apache-airflow/stable/security/index.html) — 2026-05-31 — NOTABLE (HIGH for the RCE if you run multi-tenant Airflow).

## Worth watching
- **C2 ISAC — Communications Cybersecurity Information Sharing and Analysis Center** stood up by AT&T, Charter, Comcast, Cox, Lumen, T-Mobile, Verizon and Zayo. Announced 2026-05-26 (just outside the window); operations begin June 2026 with Rich Baich (AT&T) as inaugural chair and Valerie Moon (ex-CISA/FBI) as executive director. Explicit framing is post-Salt Typhoon: cross-carrier threat-intel sharing on signalling/CALEA/network-edge intrusions that no single carrier can see end-to-end. Worth tracking for whether any technical IoC sharing actually reaches the open community. [AT&T release](https://about.att.com/story/2026/c2-isac.html), [Cybersecurity Dive](https://www.cybersecuritydive.com/news/telecom-cybersecurity-c2-isac-launch/820553/), [Industrial Cyber](https://industrialcyber.co/critical-infrastructure/us-telecom-giants-launch-c2-isac-to-strengthen-cyber-intelligence-sharing-protect-communications-infrastructure/).
- **Salt Typhoon — incomplete remediation** continues to be reported through May 2026; multiple analyses note carriers have not been able to prove eradication. No new disclosure in window but the C2 ISAC framing references it explicitly. Baseline assumption for any telco threat model: long-dwell PRC actor with router-plane persistence.
- **FortiClient EMS CVE-2026-35616 + EKZ infostealer** — Arctic Wolf write-up from 2026-05-28 continues to develop; CVE was added to CISA KEV in April. EKZ pulls Chrome/Firefox cookies (session reuse without MFA) and saved creds. Disclosed-and-patched but the campaign is still running. [Arctic Wolf](https://arcticwolf.com/resources/blog/forticlient-ems-exploited-via-cve-2026-35616-to-deliver-ekz-infostealer-disguised-as-a-fortinet-patch/), [Help Net Security](https://www.helpnetsecurity.com/2026/05/29/forticlient-ems-vulnerability-infostealer/).
- **Cisco Catalyst SD-WAN CVE-2026-20182 (CVSS 10.0)** — `vdaemon` peer-auth bypass on DTLS/UDP 12346 when peer claims `vHub`; cert validation is skipped while the peer is still marked authenticated. Limited exploitation clustered as UAT-8616 by Talos (SSH key drop, NETCONF mods, root escalation). Patched 2026-05 but still being scanned. Carriers running Catalyst SD-WAN management on internet-reachable interfaces are the obvious target set. [Cisco PSIRT advisory](https://sec.cloudapps.cisco.com/security/center/content/CiscoSecurityAdvisory/cisco-sa-sdwan-rpa2-v69WY2SW), [Talos](https://blog.talosintelligence.com/sd-wan-ongoing-exploitation/), [Rapid7](https://www.rapid7.com/blog/post/ve-cve-2026-20182-critical-authentication-bypass-cisco-catalyst-sd-wan-controller-fixed/).
- **NGINX Rift (CVE-2026-42945)** — 18-year-old heap overflow in `ngx_http_rewrite_module`, RCE on worker. Disclosed 2026-05-13, exploitation observed by VulnCheck since 2026-05-16. Patched in 1.31.1 / 1.30.1. Many telecom edge proxies and CDN frontends ship vendored NGINX, so version-pinning is the obvious thing to audit. [Help Net Security](https://www.helpnetsecurity.com/2026/05/18/ngnix-vulnerability-exploited-cve-2026-42945/), [F5 K000161019](https://my.f5.com/manage/s/article/K000161019).
- **Citizen Lab "Bad Connection" SS7/Diameter surveillance research** (2026-04-23) — still the best public attribution-ish primer on commercial surveillance abuse of interconnect. Useful baseline for any offensive-telco engagement scoping. [Citizen Lab](https://citizenlab.ca/research/uncovering-global-telecom-exploitation-by-covert-surveillance-actors/).
