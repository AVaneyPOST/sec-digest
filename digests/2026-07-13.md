# Security digest — 2026-07-13

Window: last 24h ending ~2026-07-13 04:06 UTC.

## TL;DR
- **Microsoft publicly confirms the Windows "GDID" — a persistent per-install device identifier that cannot be disabled — after it surfaced in an FBI Scattered Spider case filing.** gHacks Tech News published on 12 Jul, extending the 10 Jul Windows Latest disclosure. The GDID (64-bit MSA Device PUID minted by `login.live.com` at Microsoft-account sign-in) tracked the alleged Peter Stokes across VPN/proxy hops for eight months and four countries. No consent screen, persists across updates, only clean reinstall clears it. Attribution surface for anyone red-teaming from Windows dev VMs or standing up throw-away MS accounts.
- **jscrambler npm compromise, day 2 — 8.18.0 and 8.20.0 (the two `require()`-time dropper releases) are still live on npm and still not deprecated.** SafeDep and Socket coverage carried through 12 Jul; Security Boulevard republished the write-up on 12 Jul. `8.14/8.16/8.17` are marked deprecated-as-compromised; the two versions that bypass `--ignore-scripts` are the ones still in play. `latest` points to a clean 8.22.0. Continue purging every affected version from lockfiles and caches; treat dev-workstation secrets as burned for any install since 11 Jul.
- **Argentina Football Association hack, weekend follow-through — AFA email account used to blast "SYSTEM HACKED: UNFAIR DECISION" messages to accredited media, signed "All Egyptian Cyber Warriors".** AFA confirmed the breach on 10 Jul (post the 8 Jul Argentina-Egypt round-of-16 match); the mass emails and threats of "additional digital assaults if justice isn't delivered" rolled through 11-12 Jul. Ideologically-driven hacktivism, no ransom. Notable as a live example of BEC-adjacent tradecraft against a sports body, and as a reminder that any high-visibility corporate mailbox is a hacktivism target the moment a geopolitical / cultural flashpoint lands.
- **CitrixBleed 2 → DragonForce ransomware productised playbook, weekend wave.** Huntress' 09-10 Jul writeup on the CVE-2025-5777 → SYSTEM registry-symlink LPE → ScreenConnect/Zoho Assist persistence → DragonForce chain got carried by The Hacker News, gbhackers and Cybersecurity News on 10-11 Jul. "Under an hour from initial access to ransomware" in at least one incident. High confidence assessment: single initial-access broker running the runbook. Patch NetScaler and hunt for the same rogue-admin-name / rogue-hostname IOCs across the fleet.
- **Telecom SS7/Diameter/GTP/SIGTRAN/5G-SBA/N32/SEPP/IMS/XCAP: no fresh disclosures in-window.** GSMA, CCCS, CISA, Cisco/Ericsson/Nokia/Mavenir/Oracle Comms PSIRTs — all quiet in the 24h window. ## General high-severity
- **Microsoft confirms Windows GDID (Global Device ID) telemetry identifier — 12 Jul mainstream write-up** — [HIGH / privacy, cross-industry] gHacks Tech News published on 12 Jul; Microsoft's Azure Monitor reference table had documented the field for enterprise reporting but not surfaced to end users. FBI federal complaint against alleged Scattered Spider member Peter Stokes uses `g:6755467234350028` to link a Tzulo VPN proxy sign-up on the ngrok signup page with an accessed victim-retailer site three hours later. No consent screen; persists across Windows updates; only wiped on clean reinstall. Enterprise implication: the identifier is a new attribution surface (blue-team and law-enforcement both) that offensive teams standing up throw-away Windows VMs need to plan around. Sources: [gHacks (12 Jul)](https://www.ghacks.net/2026/07/12/microsoft-confirms-windows-gdid-device-identifier-that-cannot-be-disabled-documented-in-fbi-case-filing/), [Windows Latest (10 Jul)](https://www.windowslatest.com/2026/07/10/you-cant-fully-disable-microsofts-gdid-windows-11-tracker-but-these-settings-limit-what-it-captures/), [Cybernews](https://cybernews.com/security/windows-telemetry-gdid-helps-arrest-hacker/), [Tbreak / MSA Device PUID technical](https://tbreak.com/windows-pc-secret-id-number-microsoft-just-used/).
- **`jscrambler` npm compromise — 8.18.0 / 8.20.0 still live, still un-deprecated** — [HIGH, follow-on] Weekend coverage confirmed the two `require()`-time dropper releases that bypass `--ignore-scripts` remain published to the registry despite `latest` now pointing to a clean 8.22.0. Sources: [Security Boulevard (12 Jul)](https://securityboulevard.com/2026/07/jscrambler-npm-package-compromised-a-security-vendor-becomes-the-supply-chain-risk/), [SafeDep](https://safedep.io/jscrambler-npm-supply-chain-compromise/), [Socket](https://socket.dev/blog/jscrambler-supply-chain-attack), [Jscrambler own advisory](https://jscrambler.com/blog/security-advisory-malicious-npm-package).
