## TL;DR

- **Splunk Enterprise CVE-2026-20253 (CVSS 9.8) is now under active exploitation** — unauthenticated PostgreSQL-sidecar file write → RCE on the SIEM running your detections. CISA added it to KEV on 2026-06-18 with a federal patch deadline of Sunday 2026-06-21.

## Telecom

No first-party telecom-protocol disclosures landed in the last 24h. The closest in-window items relevant to telco offensive practice are below.

- **FortiBleed credential dataset still spreading** — Listings for the ~74k Fortinet VPN credential dump now name several telecom and ISP enterprise tenants (AT&T, Comcast and others surfaced in researcher samples). For telco red teams the practical risk is: edge VPN concentrators are the predictable pivot into OSS/BSS, lawful-intercept and roaming-OAM admin networks. Rotate, then hunt for prior interactive logons matching the leaked accounts. [bleepingcomputer.com](https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/fortibleed-leak-exposes-fortinet-vpn-credentials-for-73-000-devices/) · [helpnetsecurity.com](https://www.helpnetsecurity.com/2026/06/18/fortinet-fortibleed-data-leak/) — disclosed 2026-06-17, still developing 2026-06-19 — **[HIGH]**
- **Splunk Enterprise CVE-2026-20253 on KEV** — Many telcos run Splunk as the SIEM aggregating roaming-firewall, SEPP and 5G-SBA logs. An unauthenticated RCE on the SIEM means an attacker can suppress signalling-firewall alerts and rewrite detection logic on the box that's supposed to catch them. Treat the SIEM as a Tier-0 target. (See General high-severity for details.) — **[HIGH for telco-SOC scope]**

Nothing else in-window from GSMA, Cisco Service Provider, Ericsson, Nokia, Mavenir or Oracle Communications PSIRTs (Oracle's quarterly CPU on 2026-06-16 included 3 Communications patches but is outside the 24h window and contained no exploitation evidence).

## General high-severity

- **CVE-2026-20253 — Splunk Enterprise unauth file-write → RCE, actively exploited** — Auth-less endpoint on a Postgres sidecar lets a network-reachable attacker create/truncate arbitrary files; chains to RCE per WatchTowr's 2026-06-12 PoC. Splunk updated its advisory on 2026-06-18 confirming in-the-wild exploitation; CISA added it to KEV the same day with mitigation due **2026-06-21**. First-ever Splunk entry on KEV. Affected: Splunk Enterprise 10.0.0–10.0.6 and 10.2.0–10.2.3 (also Splunk Cloud / Secure Gateway). Fixes: 10.0.7, 10.2.4, 10.4.0, 9.3.13, 9.4.12.
