## General high-severity

- **Oracle PeopleSoft PeopleTools CVE-2026-35273 (CVSS 9.8) — unauthenticated RCE, 0-day-exploited by ShinyHunters since 2026-05-27.** Oracle published an out-of-band Security Alert on 2026-06-10 and the Mandiant/GTIG attribution dropped on 2026-06-11 along with broad press coverage. The flaw permits a remote unauthenticated attacker with HTTP/HTTPS access to PeopleSoft Internet Architecture (PIA) — i.e. the public web tier — to take over PeopleTools 8.61 and 8.62 completely (auth bypass + arbitrary code path). GTIG observed exploitation from 2026-05-27 through at least 2026-06-09 with consistent post-exploitation tooling — purpose-built `uon_fanout.sh` shell script for spreading defacement markers across PeopleSoft infrastructure, in-memory webshells, and bulk extraction from `PSOPRDEFN`, `PS_PERSONAL_DATA`, and PeopleSoft Campus Solutions student tables. 100+ organisations breached across 300 vulnerable instances; 68% higher-education; ShinyHunters has begun publishing samples to extort named victims (Nottingham 40 GB / ~450k students confirmed by the university 2026-06-11). PeopleSoft Campus Solutions and HCM are widely deployed in universities, public sector, large enterprise HR, and telco BSS — exposure is essentially "do you run PeopleTools 8.61/8.62 with a public PIA endpoint?". Mitigation: apply Oracle's 2026-06-10 emergency patch immediately; if you can't, take the PIA web tier off the public internet; rotate PeopleSoft signon tokens, integration broker creds, and any AD service accounts the App Server runs as; hunt for `uon_fanout.sh` and unexpected `/psp/` and `/psc/` 200s from outside addresses since 2026-05-27. The June 2026 Oracle Critical Patch Update is still scheduled for 2026-07-15 — this one didn't wait. [CRITICAL]

- **CISA Binding Operational Directive 26-04 — "Prioritizing Security Updates Based on Risk" — published 2026-06-11.** Replaces the uniform BOD 22-01 KEV remediation clock with a four-criteria model (Asset Exposure, KEV Status, Exploit Automation, Post-Exploitation Technical Impact). The 3-day window applies to the worst quadrant: publicly-exposed asset, KEV-listed, automatable exploit, total system control. The directive also mandates forensic triage before patching when those four conditions are met — i.e. an explicit "assume compromise" posture for the most exposed federal systems. 60 days for policy alignment, 180 days to enforce the new timelines and start continuous KEV-status reporting against asset inventory. Practical impact: in the FCEB perimeter, the BOD 22-01 14-day average for KEV becomes 3 days for the worst cases (and looser for non-exposed assets). Non-federal telcos under CIRCIA / sector-specific guidance should expect this risk model to propagate into next-cycle CISA/NSA guidance. [NOTABLE — federal policy, durable]

- **South Korea PIPC fines Coupang ₩624.68bn (~US$409–456M) for 2025 breach affecting 37M+ customers** — formal delivery on 2026-06-11. Breakdown: ₩423.6bn for the breach itself + ₩201.1bn for unauthorised collection of online activity records; Coupang Fulfillment Service hit with a separate ₩248M fine. The root-cause finding is striking — PIPC explicitly attributed the incident to inadequate management of an authentication signing key by a former Coupang engineer (who participated in building the backup auth system), plus insufficient monitoring. Not a sophisticated external compromise — an insider key-theft enabled by control gaps. Data scope: names, email, phone, delivery addresses, order history for ~33.2M members and 4.3M non-members in delivery records; the threat actor combined data across systems to build profiles before extortion. Coupang has announced it will challenge in court. Compensation programme (~₩1.685tn / ~US$1.17bn, vouchers to 33M customers) had already been announced in late 2025. Largest privacy penalty in Korean history. [HIGH — regulatory, durable signal on insider key management]
