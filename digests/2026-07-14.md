# Security digest — 2026-07-14

Window: last 24h ending ~2026-07-14 04:05 UTC.

## TL;DR
- **CISA/NSA joint advisory AA26-194A (13 Jul) — Russian FSB Center 16 exploiting poorly-configured routers worldwide, decade-plus campaign.** Broad international co-seal (Australia, Canada, NZ, UK, Czechia, Denmark, Estonia, Finland, France, Italy, Poland, Sweden). Targets = SNMPv1/v2c, weak creds, unpatched firmware, unnecessary services on edge and carrier-adjacent routers. Named the FSB unit publicly (16th Center) — the same organisation that runs Berserk Bear / Dragonfly and, per public reporting, overlaps operationally with Salt Typhoon TTPs on Cisco IOS-XE. Explicitly calls out disabling Cisco Smart Install as a compensating control. Directly telecom-adjacent for anyone with router estate at the ISP / MSSP / carrier-transport layer.
- **EU and UK sanctions targeting FSB / GRU cyber operatives, published same day (13 Jul).** EU package: 9 individuals + 4 entities; explicitly names FSB 16th Center and GRU Unit 29155's technical enabler "Impuls" (run by Yevgeny Bashev). UK ran a parallel 24-entity list. Nine EU countries named as victims of the tenure-2010 campaign. Sanctions plus AA26-194A are coordinated: the advisory tells defenders what to do, the sanctions tell the FSB/GRU there is now a paper trail.
- **Progress ShareFile — "credible external security threat" now confirmed on the customer-service side as of 12-13 Jul; still no CVE.** As of 17:00 ET 12 Jul, ShareFile cloud is back online for SZC customers but SZCs themselves must remain OFF pending Progress' investigation. The Register (13 Jul), Help Net Security (13 Jul), Cybernews all carried follow-through. The February CVE-2026-2699 (auth bypass, 9.9) and CVE-2026-2701 (arbitrary upload, 9.1) are explicitly not being linked to this incident by Progress. Rolling emergency.
- **Nihon Kotsu — Japan's largest taxi operator — confirmed malware infection 11 Jul, publicly disclosed 13 Jul.** Dispatch systems still offline; no ransomware group has claimed. No confirmed data theft yet. Japan's dispatch/back-office ecosystem sits on top of a carrier-integrated mobile-signalling stack; the incident is telecom-adjacent even where the attack surface itself isn't SS7/IMS.
- **Microsoft Patch Tuesday tomorrow (14 Jul) + Kerberos RC4 hardening enforcement deadline tomorrow.** July batch is previewed at ~127 CVEs; the KB rolling out at 18:00 UTC 14 Jul permanently removes the `RC4DefaultDisablementPhase` rollback. Any Kerberos service account still requesting RC4 will start failing authentication the moment the update lands on the DC. Watch also whether Nightmare-Eclipse follows through on the (partially-walked-back) 14 Jul "bone-shattering" drop.

## Telecom
- **CISA / NSA AA26-194A — "Improve Router Hygiene to Protect Against Russian State-Sponsored Targeting"** — [HIGH, telecom-adjacent] Published 13 Jul; TTPs are opportunistic-scan → weak SNMP / outdated firmware / default creds → persistent GRE/OSPF re-routing and traffic-mirroring. Practitioner-level items called out: disable Cisco Smart Install (CVE-2018-0171 era), disable SNMPv1/v2c, remove unnecessary services (telnet, http admin), rotate creds not touched in the last two years. For a telecom offensive-security practitioner this is a live target-list of what the FSB is spraying against — GRE-tunnel implants and RCM (Router Configuration Modification) are named. The joint advisory is the first time a Western coalition has attributed this exact campaign to the FSB 16th Center as a named unit. Sources: [CISA AA26-194A](https://www.cisa.gov/news-events/cybersecurity-advisories/aa26-194a), [NSA press release](https://www.nsa.gov/Press-Room/Press-Releases-Statements/Press-Release-View/Article/4541059/nsa-and-partners-release-guidance-on-improving-router-hygiene-to-protect-agains/), [Cybersecurity News](https://cyberpress.org/nsa-warns-poorly-configured-routers-are-fueling-russian-state-sponsored-cyberattacks/), [DOD-hosted CSA PDF](https://media.defense.gov/2026/Jul/09/2003959498/-1/-1/0/CSA_IMPROVE_ROUTER_HYGIENE.PDF).
- **Nihon Kotsu — Japan's largest taxi / hire-vehicle operator, dispatch platform offline** — [NOTABLE] Malware infection detected in the early hours of 11 Jul, publicly disclosed 13 Jul. 8,558 taxis and ~2,000 chauffeur vehicles routed through the offline dispatch platform, which fronts a mixed IP + PSTN dispatch stack integrated with major Japanese MNOs. No ransom claim as of write time. Dispatch back-office is the primary attack surface, not the carrier link itself, but this is a live example of how a telco-integrated logistics platform going down cascades into carrier-visible outages (call routing to dispatch, driver PTT, in-vehicle SIM/eSIM signalling). Sources: [BleepingComputer](https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/japans-largest-taxi-operator-shuts-systems-after-cyberattack/), [TechNadu](https://www.technadu.com/nihon-kotsu-confirms-malware-infection-taxi-dispatch-systems-disrupted/630850/).
- **Tenda router CVE-2026-11405 — undocumented authentication backdoor, still unpatched** — [HIGH, rolling] CERT/CC coordinated disclosure (VU#213560) rolled into general-press coverage across 12-13 Jul. `login()` in `/bin/httpd` falls through to a hidden `sys.rzadmin.password` path that compares plaintext directly. Affected: FH1201, W15E, AC10, AC5, AC6v2 firmware trains at minimum. No patch — CERT/CC notified Tenda 19 May, no response. Not carrier equipment, but Tenda is heavily deployed at the last-mile CPE layer in Latin America, Southeast Asia and parts of Europe; a fixed backdoor password becomes an ISP-wide pivot the moment it's extracted from a single unit. Sources: [CERT/CC VU#213560](https://kb.cert.org/vuls/id/213560), [The Hacker News](https://thehackernews.com/2026/07/certcc-warns-of-hidden-admin-backdoor.html), [BleepingComputer](https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/hidden-backdoor-in-tenda-router-firmware-grants-admin-access/), [Security Affairs](https://securityaffairs.com/194878/security/hidden-tenda-router-backdoor-grants-admin-access-no-patch-available.html).
