## Telecom

- **FBI / CISA PSA I-062626-PSA — Russian intelligence pivots to Signal Backup
  Recovery Keys** *(PSA dated 26 Jun 2026, broad reporting Sat 27 Jun, HIGH — APT /
  messaging)*
  The updated PSA supersedes the March-26 FBI/CISA advisory and is the live story of
  the weekend. Operators continue to impersonate Signal support and now coach the
  victim through the Backup → Settings flow to lift the Backup Recovery Key. Once the
  attacker holds the key they can restore the encrypted message backup to a device they
  control and read historical 1:1 and group conversations — including content from
  before the phish. The PSA flags that **creating a new Signal account on the same
  phone number does *not* invalidate the stolen recovery key**, so phone-number reuse
  is not a recovery path. Two new public tracking clusters: UNC5792 (FSB-linked) and
  UNC4221 (military intelligence). Targets remain government officials, military,
  political figures, journalists, and Ukraine-aligned activists in the US, EU, and
  Ukraine. Telco-practitioner angle: SMS-delivered lures, fake "support" identities,
  and exploitation of the assumption that SMS-to-app onboarding flows are
  authoritative — operators running A2P / messaging-aggregation visibility should
  hunt for the impersonation patterns and consider whether their carrier-side
  registration-binding telemetry could detect re-registration of compromised handles.
  [The Hacker News: https://thehackernews.com/2026/06/fbi-warns-russian-intelligence-hackers.html]
  [BleepingComputer: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/fbi-russian-hackers-now-target-signal-backup-recovery-keys/]
  [Security Affairs: https://securityaffairs.com/194360/intelligence/new-fbi-alert-russian-intelligence-uses-signal-recovery-keys-to-access-messages.html]

- **Cisco UCM CVE-2026-20230 — KEV/BOD-26-04 patching deadline today (Sun 28 Jun)**
  *(KEV-added 25 Jun, deadline 28 Jun, HIGH — VoIP / UC)*
  SSRF in the WebDialer service on Unified Communications Manager / UCM Session
  Management Edition. Unauthenticated attacker sends a file:// payload, gets arbitrary
  file write on the underlying OS, escalates to root on the VoIP control plane.
  Defused has been observing live exploitation from a single source IP since the
  weekend of 21–22 Jun. WebDialer is disabled by default — enterprises that turned it
  on for click-to-call are the exposed population. Telco / managed-voice ops should
  treat the today-deadline as the operational tripwire: either Cisco fixed release
  applied, or WebDialer disabled, or the host is post-deadline non-compliant under
  the 3-day BOD-26-04 mandate. Same Sunday-EOD deadline applies to **Splunk
  Enterprise CVE-2026-20253** (PostgreSQL-sidecar arbitrary write, CVSS 9.8).
  [BleepingComputer: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/cisco-unified-cm-sme-flaw-cve-2026-20230-now-exploited-in-attacks/]
  [The Hacker News: https://thehackernews.com/2026/06/cisco-unified-cm-flaw-exploited-after.html]
  [SecurityWeek: https://www.securityweek.com/hackers-exploiting-cisco-unified-cm-vulnerability/]
  [CISA KEV add: https://www.cisa.gov/news-events/alerts/2026/06/25/cisa-adds-two-known-exploited-vulnerabilities-catalog]

- **SpaceX S-1 / IPO road-show recasts Starlink Mobile as a terrestrial-MNO
  competitor** *(26 Jun 2026, NOTABLE — strategy / threat-model)*
  Not a security disclosure, but it materially changes the telecom adversary surface
  worth tracking. SpaceX's prospectus and Gwynne Shotwell's investor remarks (26 Jun
  roadshow) describe Starlink Mobile as a direct-to-smartphone wireless service
  "on par with terrestrial mobile networks", with the May-26 FCC-approved EchoStar
  spectrum purchase (40 MHz AWS-4 + 15 MHz AWS-3 + 10 MHz H-Block, 65 MHz total,
  flex-use waiver) providing licensed mid-band capacity for hybrid space-terrestrial
  use. Shotwell confirmed SpaceX is "considering" building its own US land-based
  cellular network — i.e. towers, base stations, core. For an offensive-security
  practitioner the watch-items are (a) a new SBA / N32 peering surface as Starlink
  Mobile establishes roaming with terrestrial MNOs, (b) SIM-OTA / eUICC profile
  download flows for a satellite-direct service, (c) lawful-intercept architecture
  for a globally-reachable handset population, and (d) any operator collateral or
  third-party PSIRT disclosure around the EchoStar spectrum-transition migration.
  [SpaceNews S-1 readout: https://spacenews.com/spacex-prospectus-casts-starlink-mobile-as-future-wireless-challenger/]

- **Microsoft UEFI CA 2011 Secure Boot certificate expired 27 Jun on schedule**
  *(HIGH — fleet management, operational carry-over)*
  Yesterday's headline; today is the first calendar day with fleets in the
  post-expiry state. The forward risk is unchanged: devices that haven't received
  the 2023 CA updates (KEK CA 2023, UEFI CA 2023, Windows UEFI CA 2023) keep
  booting but cannot accept new bootloader / SBAT / DBX updates and will not trust
  newly-signed third-party shims. First business day of post-expiry telemetry is
  tomorrow (Mon 29 Jun); expect distro and OEM advisories to surface fleet-coverage
  gaps. Telco impact areas: management servers, MEC nodes, OEM appliances on
  firmware older than early-2025.
  [Microsoft Support: https://support.microsoft.com/en-us/topic/windows-secure-boot-certificate-expiration-and-ca-updates-7ff40d33-95dc-4c3c-8725-a9b95457578e]

- **Carry-over: DirtyClone / DirtyFrag CVE-2026-43503 — Linux LPE via cloned
  packets** *(JFrog write-up 25 Jun, HIGH)*
  Mainline fix in v7.1-rc5 (commit 9e171fc1d7d7) shipped 21 May. Holders of
  CAP_NET_ADMIN — frequently obtainable via unprivileged user namespaces — can
  escalate to root by abusing skb cloning across an attacker-controlled IPsec
  tunnel. Highest exposure: multi-tenant cloud, K8s, privileged containers, telco
  Linux fleets that haven't picked up the May LTS backports. Temporary mitigation:
  `kernel.unprivileged_userns_clone=0`.
  [JFrog research: https://research.jfrog.com/post/dissecting-and-exploiting-linux-lpe-variant-dirtyclone-cve-2026-43503/]
  [The Hacker News: https://thehackernews.com/2026/06/new-dirtyclone-linux-kernel-flaw-lets.html]

## Worth watching

- **Gentlemen RaaS / GentleKiller EDR-killer suite — ESET / Group-IB / Check Point
  consolidation reporting (25–26 Jun).** Centralised, in-house EDR-killer framework
  with 8+ vulnerable-driver variants targeting 400+ processes across ~48 security
  products. Affiliates served a packaged kit rather than DIY tooling. Internal
  leaks confirm zeta88 / hastalamuerte (gang lead) maintains the suite. Globally
  distributed victimology with notable SE-Asia / South America / Western-Europe
  concentration; under-represented in US incidents. Telco angle: managed-services
  providers and outsourced telco IT shops in those regions are squarely in the
  victim profile.
  [ESET WeLiveSecurity: https://www.welivesecurity.com/en/eset-research/killing-me-gently-inside-gentlemens-edr-killer-framework/]
  [BleepingComputer: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/gentlemen-ransomware-uses-multiple-edr-killers-to-disable-defenses/]
  [The Hacker News: https://thehackernews.com/2026/06/the-gentlemen-raas-uses-gentlekiller.html]

- **macOS Gaslight Rust backdoor with embedded prompt-injection (25–26 Jun,
  SentinelOne).** DPRK-aligned implant whose key novelty is a Markdown-fenced cascade
  of 38 fabricated "system" messages — token expiry, OOM kills, disk exhaustion —
  designed to convince an LLM-assisted triage agent to abort or refuse analysis.
  Capabilities are standard (Telegram-bot C2 polling shell, Chrome / Brave / Firefox
  / Safari data, login keychain, terminal history, installed-apps). The interesting
  bit for defenders is the prompt-injection-vs-AI-triage technique: any internal
  pipeline that auto-summarises samples through an LLM is now a credible attack
  surface, including SOC tooling at telco SOCs.
  [The Hacker News: https://thehackernews.com/2026/06/new-gaslight-macos-malware-uses-prompt.html]
  [BleepingComputer: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/new-macos-malware-embeds-fake-errors-to-confuse-ai-analysis-tools/]
  [Infosecurity Magazine: https://www.infosecurity-magazine.com/news/macos-gaslight-rust-backdoor/]

  [Cybernews: https://cybernews.com/security/polymarket-hit-by-cyberattack-via-third-party-dependency/]
