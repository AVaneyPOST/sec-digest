## Telecom

## General high-severity

- **Splunk CVE-2026-20253 — CISA KEV deadline expired Sunday 2026-06-21** *(KEV-added 2026-06-18; deadline 2026-06-21 EOD)* — No extension granted; agencies that haven't patched to 10.2.4 / 10.0.7 or disabled the PG sidecar are now in formal BOD 26-04 non-compliance. WatchTowr reporting through the weekend (relayed by The Hacker News on Sunday) names **UNC6508** (China-linked) as one of the operators actively chaining the unauth file-write to PostgreSQL-side RCE. First Splunk flaw ever on KEV. Continue treating any Splunk management plane reachable from internet or attacker-controlled VLAN as in-scope. [Help Net Security](https://www.helpnetsecurity.com/2026/06/19/splunk-vulnerability-cve-2026-20253-exploited/) · [SecurityWeek](https://www.securityweek.com/splunk-enterprise-vulnerability-exploited-in-attacks-days-after-disclosure/) · [SecurityAffairs (KEV addition)](https://securityaffairs.com/193888/security/u-s-cisa-adds-splunk-enterprise-flaw-to-its-known-exploited-vulnerabilities-catalog-and-urges-agencies-to-fix-it-by-sunday.html) · **CRITICAL**
- **Prinz Eugen ransomware — Go-based, hands-on-keyboard, no ransom note, prioritizes recent files** *(BleepingComputer 2026-06-21; underlying ThreatDown/Malwarebytes research published 2026-06-20)* — Initial access via stolen RDP, dropper `servertool.exe`, ChaCha20-Poly1305 with Argon2id+HKDF-SHA256 key derivation, 1 MB chunked encryption, recursion that targets recently-modified files first. Operators abuse legit RMM (AnyDesk-class tools) and LOLBins; not run as RaaS, no affiliate recruitment yet. Five confirmed victims; Standard Bank Group refused a 1 BTC demand and is now on the group's leak site (3-week dwell, lateral movement through SharePoint / OneDrive / Power Apps / AppDynamics / Jira / Confluence / Citrix / Remedy / MSSQL / Oracle DB). Detection angle: the absence of a ransom note plus the recency-bias selector are detectable signals on host file-modification telemetry. [BleepingComputer](https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/new-prinz-eugen-ransomware-prioritizes-recent-files-for-encryption/) · [ThreatDown research](https://www.threatdown.com/blog/prinz-eugen-ransomware-a-deep-dive-into-a-new-go-based-encryptor/) · **HIGH**

## Worth watching

- **Mastra npm supply-chain (Sapphire Sleet / BlueNoroff)** — Microsoft attribution published 2026-06-17; 144 packages republished in an 88-minute automated campaign with a typosquatted `easy-day-js` dependency, leveraging an unrevoked `ehindero` org-scope token. Post-install script disables TLS verification, fetches second-stage from C2, drops PowerShell backdoor + SYSTEM service for persistence. NPM scope hygiene is the lesson; any pipeline that pins `@mastra/*` between 2026-06-17 01:12 UTC and the maintainer cleanup needs to be considered compromised. [Microsoft Security Blog](https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/security/blog/2026/06/17/postinstall-payload-inside-mastra-npm-supply-chain-compromise/) · [BleepingComputer (attribution update 2026-06-19)](https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/microsoft-links-mastra-ai-supply-chain-attack-to-north-korean-hackers/)

[The Hacker News](https://thehackernews.com/2026/06/operation-endgame-disrupts-socgholish.html) · [Help Net Security](https://www.helpnetsecurity.com/2026/06/18/law-enforcement-socgholish-operation-endgame/)
