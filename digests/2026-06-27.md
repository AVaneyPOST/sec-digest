## General high-severity

- **Microsoft UEFI CA 2011 Secure Boot certificate expires today** *(27 Jun 2026, HIGH — fleet management)*
  The Microsoft Corporation UEFI CA 2011 — used to sign third-party bootloaders including the Linux shim — reaches end of validity today. The earlier Microsoft Corporation KEK CA 2011 expired on 24 Jun; the Windows Production PCA 2011 follows on 19 Oct. Devices that already booted continue booting; the operational risk is forward — once the 2011 CAs are out, any system that hasn't received the 2023 CA updates (Microsoft Corporation KEK CA 2023, Microsoft UEFI CA 2023, Microsoft Windows UEFI CA 2023) loses the ability to receive future bootloader / SBAT / DBX updates, and newly-signed third-party shims will not be trusted. The transition has been telegraphed by Microsoft, Dell, ASUS, and the major Linux distros since late 2025, but coverage at scale is uneven — practitioners should query fleets for the presence of the 2023 certs in `db` and `KEK` and triage devices still relying on the 2011 chain. Telco impact: management servers, MEC nodes, and OEM appliances in racks that haven't received OEM firmware updates since early 2025 are the most likely to be stuck. [Microsoft Support: https://support.microsoft.com/en-us/topic/windows-secure-boot-certificate-expiration-and-ca-updates-7ff40d33-95dc-4c3c-8725-a9b95457578e] [Microsoft Tech Community playbook: https://techcommunity.microsoft.com/blog/windows-itpro-blog/secure-boot-playbook-for-certificates-expiring-in-2026/4469235] [Dell KB: https://www.dell.com/support/kbdoc/en-us/000390990/secure-boot-transition-faq] [GCP guidance: https://docs.cloud.google.com/compute/docs/security/ms-secure-boot-certificates-expiration]

- **PTC Windchill CVE-2026-12569 — RCE, added to CISA KEV** *(26 Jun 2026, HIGH)*
  CISA added Thursday 26 Jun. CVSS 9.3 improper input validation → unauthenticated RCE via crafted HTTP request to Windchill PDMlink / FlexPLM. PTC alerted customers on 17 Jun, patched over the following two days, and on 25 Jun confirmed "continued reports of heightened threat activity" with attackers dropping persistent JSP web shells for remote command execution and data exfiltration. This is the first PTC vulnerability ever in KEV. Coverage notes German federal police mobilised to alert affected operators. FCEB BOD 22-01 deadline for federal patching is 17 Jul 2026. Practitioner angle: Windchill is widely used in aerospace, automotive, and defence-supplier estates — credential reuse and lateral-movement risk into adjacent OT networks is the real downside. [CISA KEV add: https://www.cisa.gov/news-events/alerts/2026/06/25/cisa-adds-two-known-exploited-vulnerabilities-catalog] [The Hacker News: https://thehackernews.com/2026/06/cisa-adds-exploited-ptc-windchill-rce.html] [SecurityWeek: https://www.securityweek.com/first-ever-exploitation-of-ptc-windchill-vulnerability-discovered-in-the-wild/amp/] [SecurityWeek German-police angle: https://www.securityweek.com/cisa-flags-critical-ptc-vulnerability-that-had-german-police-mobilized/] [PTC advisory: https://www.ptc.com/en/about/trust-center/advisory-center/active-advisories/windchill-flexplm-rce-vulnerability]

- **Turla / Secret Blizzard — STOCKSTAY .NET backdoor disclosed** *(Google TIG report, 26 Jun 2026, HIGH — APT)*
  Google Threat Intelligence Group attributes a previously-undocumented .NET / WinForms backdoor named STOCKSTAY to Turla (Secret Blizzard, FSB Centre 16), deployed against Ukrainian government and military targets and entities with Italian foreign-policy interests, with earlier waves in the Netherlands, Poland, Germany. Multi-component implant communicating over secure WebSockets via the open-source `websocket-sharp` library. Significant code and functional overlap with Kazuar (Turla's mainstay since 2017). Suspected dev activity since Dec 2022. A Nov-25 phishing wave used RAR archives exploiting WinRAR CVE-2025-8088 — the same path used by Sandworm, Gamaredon, and RomCom. Defender play: cluster on WinRAR-08088 exploitation, .NET assemblies bundling `websocket-sharp`, and the academic / diplomatic-themed lures GTIG documents. [GTIG blog: https://cloud.google.com/blog/topics/threat-intelligence/stockstay-turla-intelligence-gathering] [The Hacker News: https://thehackernews.com/2026/06/google-details-turlas-new-stockstay.html] [The Record: https://therecord.media/russia-turla-espionage-ukraine-stockstay-malware] [SC Media: https://www.scworld.com/brief/turla-group-deploys-new-stockstay-backdoor-against-ukraine-and-italy]
