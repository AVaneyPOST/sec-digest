Practitioner relevance: FortiSandbox is the sandboxing tier that several Tier-1 carriers and managed-SOC providers run in their perimeter and email-security stack. Loss of the sandbox itself is bad on three counts — credential / config exfil, malicious-sample exfiltration, and integrity loss for downstream verdicts. Audit ingress to `/jsonrpc/`, confirm 5.0.6 / 4.4.9 / 4.2.7+ patch lines on every appliance including FortiSandbox Cloud tenants. ## General high-severity

- **CISA KEV addition 2026-06-16 — CVE-2026-48907 (Joomla JCE Editor, CVSS 10.0, unauth RCE) [HIGH, federal-mandated].** Widget Factory Joomla Content Editor before 2.9.99.5 (mySites.guru's "JCE", one of the most widely installed Joomla extensions). Combined access-control / file-validation flaw lets an unauthenticated attacker (1) create a malicious editor profile, (2) re-enable .php and .txt uploads via that profile, (3) drop a webshell. PoC has been public on GitHub since 2026-06-09; the developer has shipped 2.9.99.5 (initial fix) and 2.9.99.6 (hardening). Federal civilian agencies must remediate by 2026-07-07 under BOD 26-04. Practitioner relevance: low for the signalling plane; meaningful for any Joomla-based operator portal, MVNO sales site, marketing or partner portal — Joomla shops are quietly common in MVNO / regional-carrier land. Inventory and patch. ## Worth watching

- **Rokarolla Android banker (Zimperium zLabs, blog dated 2026-06-16).** New banking trojan, 217 banking & cryptocurrency apps targeted, ~137-command C2 vocabulary; capabilities include lock-screen-PIN capture, full SMS read/send (the SMS-OTP-interception path that still keeps SMS-based 2FA dangerous), keylogging, clipboard rewrite to redirect crypto payments, and disabling Google Play Protect. Distribution: malicious sites masquerading as Google Chrome and TikTok download pages; dropper poses as "Google Play Protect". Practitioner takeaway: mobile-banking-trojan families with SMS-stealing built in remain the single biggest reason operators still see fraud against legitimate SMS-OTP flows; this one has IOCs on GitHub. Worth flagging into MNO fraud and operator-customer-comms teams. - **Verizon VoLTE IPsec / IMS SIP-signalling integrity issue — CVE-2026-10629, CERT/CC VU#615987 (disclosed ~2026-06-10).** Not in window today but the most relevant signalling-plane disclosure of the month and still active: SIP REGISTER / INVITE / MESSAGE / BYE / UPDATE on Verizon's IMS observed without ESP-encapsulated IPsec and without SIP Security Agreement headers (3GPP TS 33.203). On-path attacker can intercept, replay, or modify VoLTE signalling — call hijack, spoofing, DoS, emergency-call routing manipulation. Researchers DongWon Lee / Jeongmin Choi / CheolJun Park (Kyung Hee University) coordinated through CERT/CC. Verizon initially engaged then withdrew from coordination and self-classified "Not Affected" on the basis that the GSMA / 3GPP language is permissive rather than mandatory; CERT/CC kept the note public. Apple's iOS 26.5 carrier bundle (released 2026-05-11) added IMS-IPsec carrier-config entries — useful as a forward-looking client lever but does not confirm Verizon-side negotiation in production. Worth tracking for further vendor / regulator pressure and for whether AT&T / T-Mobile / Vodafone / Telefónica disclosures follow. [CERT/CC VU#615987](https://kb.cert.org/vuls/id/615987) · [CyberInsider write-up](https://cyberinsider.com/verizon-volte-network-found-missing-ipsec-protections-for-sip-signaling/) · [CyberPress — Attackers can manipulate SIP messages in Verizon VoLTE](https://cyberpress.org/verizon-volte-sip-manipulation/).
