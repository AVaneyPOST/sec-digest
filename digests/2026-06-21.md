## Telecom

- **Meta vs NSO Group — fresh Pegasus campaign on WhatsApp, contempt motion filed** *(2026-06-19)* — Meta's threat-intel team flagged 23 WhatsApp accounts and 34 groups created Jan–Jun 2026 linked to NSO, used to lure targets with 1-click phishing into Pegasus delivery. Meta is asking the US court to hold NSO in contempt of last year's injunction. For an offensive-telco lens: confirms surveillance vendors are still iterating client-side mobile-messaging vectors despite legal heat; expect renewed scrutiny on operator MMSI/SMS/RCS paths used for delivery. [The Hacker News](https://thehackernews.com/2026/06/meta-blocks-nso-groups-new-whatsapp.html) · [Citizen Lab](https://citizenlab.ca/whatsapp-accuses-nso-of-fresh-pegasus-targeting/) · **NOTABLE**
- **FortiBleed campaign — 86,644 FortiGates confirmed compromised, 194 countries (incl. telcos)** *(scoreboard updated 2026-06-19; CISA alert 2026-06-18)* — Validated SSL-VPN / admin creds for ~50% of internet-reachable FortiGates, harvested via cracking weak SHA-256 hashes left after upgrades. CISA urges termination of all SSL-VPN/admin sessions, password resets, phishing-resistant MFA, log review for lateral movement. Telco IT and signalling-adjacent edge GWs are squarely in scope where FortiGate is the perimeter. [CISA](https://www.cisa.gov/news-events/alerts/2026/06/18/cisa-urges-hardening-fortinet-devices-after-reports-credential-exposure) · [BleepingComputer](https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/cisa-warns-fortinet-users-to-secure-devices-after-fortibleed-leak/) · [SecurityWeek](https://www.securityweek.com/fortibleed-86000-fortinet-device-credentials-compromised/) · **HIGH**
- **Apple BootROM exploit "usbliter8" (A12/A13/S4/S5) — full PoC public** *(disclosed 2026-06-18/19)* — Paradigm Shift's writeup landed: checkm8-class BootROM compromise via a Synopsys DesignWare USB controller bug + DFU memory corruption, completes in <2s with an RP2350 board. Unpatchable in hardware. Telco-relevance: cheap, reliable physical extraction of credentials, eSIM profiles, and IMS keys from iPhone XR/XS/11/SE2 and the linked Apple Watches; SIM-swap and lawful-intercept toolchains will fold this in fast. [The Hacker News](https://thehackernews.com/2026/06/unpatchable-usbliter8-exploit-breaks.html) · [The Register](https://www.theregister.com/security/2026/06/19/researchers-drop-checkm8-style-bootrom-exploit-for-a12-and-a13-iphones/5259028) · [CyberInsider](https://cyberinsider.com/unpatchable-bootrom-exploit-for-apple-a12-a13-chips-now-public/) · **HIGH**

## General high-severity

- **Splunk Enterprise CVE-2026-20253 — CISA KEV deadline 2026-06-21** *(KEV-added 2026-06-18; active exploitation confirmed 2026-06-18/19)* — CVSS 9.8 missing-auth on a PostgreSQL sidecar endpoint → unauth arbitrary file create/truncate → RCE. Affects Splunk Enterprise 10.2 < 10.2.4 and 10.0 < 10.0.7 (patches were released 2026-06-10; PoC public 2026-06-12; in-the-wild abuse <1 week later). First Splunk flaw ever on KEV. Disable the PG sidecar or patch immediately — any SOC running Splunk should treat its own management plane as in-scope. [Help Net Security](https://www.helpnetsecurity.com/2026/06/19/splunk-vulnerability-cve-2026-20253-exploited/) · [Splunk advisory SVD-2026-0603](https://advisory.splunk.com/advisories/SVD-2026-0603) · [BleepingComputer](https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/cisa-splunk-enterprise-flaw-actively-exploited-patch-by-sunday/) · **CRITICAL**
