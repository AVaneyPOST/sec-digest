# Security digest — 2026-07-17

Window: last 24h ending ~2026-07-17 04:06 UTC.

## TL;DR
- **Hugging Face discloses an intrusion driven end-to-end by an autonomous AI-agent framework (16 Jul).** A malicious dataset abused two code-execution paths in HF's dataset processing to land on a worker, from which an agentic attacker executed ~17,000 recorded actions across short-lived sandboxes, harvested cloud/cluster credentials, and moved laterally into several internal clusters. Public models, datasets, Spaces, and the published container/package supply chain are reported clean. First widely-reported "agent-driven" intrusion in a mainstream production SaaS; sets a precedent for detection engineering.
- **Two Scattered Spider members (Thalha Jubair, 20; Owen Flowers, 18) sentenced to 5.5 years each at Woolwich Crown Court (16 Jul) for the 2024 £29M TfL hack.** First successful UK prosecutions under Computer Misuse Act Section 3ZA (unauthorised act creating a significant risk of serious damage). Notable because the group's playbook — social-engineering carrier employees / help-desks for SIM swaps and IDP resets — is the same one used against UK/US retail and cloud victims through 2025-26.
- **Symantec publishes fresh Daxin activity in Taiwan alongside a previously undocumented pre-login SYSTEM-level backdoor "Stupig" (16 Jul).** Kernel-mode rootkit last publicly seen in 2022 resurfaces on a Taiwan-based subsidiary of a multinational high-tech manufacturer. Stupig registers as a keyboard-layout provider in the Windows logon process for SYSTEM RCE + credential theft before user sign-in — an access path most EDR watch lists don't cover. Attributed to a China-linked actor; telemetry started 12 May 2026 but binaries carry 2013 compile timestamps.
- **Oracle E-Business Suite CVE-2026-46817 (CVSS 9.8, unauth account takeover via File Transmission in Oracle Payments) — CISA BOD 26-04 mitigation deadline is 18 Jul (Saturday), 3-day window instead of the usual 21 days.** Advisory reporting refreshed 16 Jul highlighting the compressed deadline. Shadowserver puts >1,000 internet-exposed Oracle EBS instances, >50% in the US. Federal agencies remediating tomorrow; enterprise EBS estates on 12.2.3-12.2.15 without the May 2026 CPU should treat as active.
- **Sophos "State of Ransomware 2026" (survey of 2,158 orgs, 15-16 Jul coverage): identity-based intrusion overtakes exploits as the top ransomware root cause.** Malicious email (26%) + phishing (24%) → half of all ransomware initial access; vulnerability exploitation drops to 18% (from 32%). 79% of ransomware attacks now start with compromised identities; 97% of the compromised-credential victims *had* MFA — pointing to partial coverage and bypass techniques as the operative gap.

- **Daxin + Stupig — China-linked APT resurfaces at Taiwan high-tech manufacturer** — [HIGH, telecom-adjacent] Not a carrier target but relevant to any telco with China-vs-Taiwan supplier / transit exposure. Daxin (`srt64.sys`) is a kernel-mode rootkit that hijacks inbound TCP flows for C2 rather than beaconing outbound — invisible to typical carrier egress filters. Stupig is a Windows Local Security Authority (LSA) plug-in registered as a keyboard-layout provider that executes attacker code at SYSTEM inside `winlogon.exe` before any user has logged in. Symantec assesses 13-year potential dwell time on the compromised environment. Practitioner take: for telco engineering-lab / R&D / OSS-BSS environments running Windows domain-joined systems, this is a fresh reason to hunt for `srt64.sys`, unexpected keyboard-layout providers, and inbound-only C2 patterns. Sources: [Symantec / Broadcom threat intel](https://www.security.com/threat-intelligence/daxin-returns-stupig), [The Hacker News](https://thehackernews.com/2026/07/daxin-resurfaces-in-taiwan-alongside.html), [GBHackers](https://gbhackers.com/stupig-system-level-malware/), [SC Media brief](https://www.scworld.com/brief/daxin-malware-resurfaces-with-new-backdoor-targeting-taiwan-manufacturer).

## General high-severity
- **Hugging Face security incident (16 Jul disclosure) — autonomous AI-agent framework runs an end-to-end intrusion into production infrastructure** — [HIGH] Root cause: two independent code-execution paths in HF's dataset processing pipeline triggered by a malicious dataset. Attacker escalated from processing worker → node-level access → cluster credentials → lateral movement into "several internal clusters." Attacker took actions across a swarm of short-lived sandboxes with self-migrating C2 staged on public services. HF says: (a) public models, datasets, Spaces untampered; (b) container images and published packages verified clean; (c) initial detection was by their own LLM-based triage over telemetry, and forensic analysis of the ~17,000-event action log was itself agent-driven. LLM behind the attacker framework is not identified. Practitioner take: this is the first mainstream production-SaaS intrusion HF explicitly attributes to an agentic framework; the industry has been forecasting this scenario, and the detection/forensic asymmetry (human vs. thousands of automated events) is the pain point to instrument for. Sources: [Hugging Face security incident blog](https://huggingface.co/blog/security-incident-july-2026).
- **Oracle E-Business Suite CVE-2026-46817 — CISA BOD 26-04 deadline 18 Jul (Saturday); 16 Jul reporting cycle emphasises the 3-day compression** — [CRITICAL, exposed EBS estates] Unauth network attack against the File Transmission component of Oracle Payments in EBS 12.2.3-12.2.15 → account takeover. Patched in May 2026 CPU. KEV addition 15 Jul with a 3-day due date vs. the usual 21 — CISA signalling live exploitation and low-friction weaponisation. Defused Cyber first flagged in-the-wild activity 29 Jun; Shadowserver counts >1,000 exposed EBS instances (majority US). No enterprise-side vendor advisory refresh from Oracle in-window; Q3 CPU due 21 Jul may include follow-ons. Sources: [BleepingComputer (16 Jul)](https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/cisa-orders-feds-to-patch-actively-exploited-oracle-flaw-by-saturday/), [CISA KEV alert (15 Jul)](https://www.cisa.gov/news-events/alerts/2026/07/15/cisa-adds-two-known-exploited-vulnerabilities-catalog), [SOCRadar](https://socradar.io/blog/cve-2026-46817-oracle-payments-takeover/), [Beazley Security advisory](https://beazley.security/alerts-advisories/critical-vulnerability-in-oracle-payments-product-under-active-exploitation-cve-2026-46817), [Cybersecurity News](https://cybersecuritynews.com/oracle-e-business-suite-vulnerability-exploited/).
- **CVE-2026-56164 SharePoint Server missing-auth EoP — BOD 26-04 deadline is today (17 Jul) for federal agencies; CISA hardening advisory guidance still current** — [CRITICAL] KEV addition was 14 Jul with the tight 3-day window ending today. NVD scores 9.8 vs. Microsoft's 5.3 — the operational reality is unauth network privilege escalation with no user interaction. CISA guidance: hunt for IIS machine-key theft first, rotate keys second, patch third (rotating first destroys the artefact needed for scoping post-exploit persistence). Enable AMSI Full Mode. Do not expose SharePoint Central Administration to the internet. Chained-exploitation cluster is CVE-2026-32201 (April KEV) → CVE-2026-45659 (1 Jul KEV) → CVE-2026-56164 (14 Jul KEV); watch for CVE-2026-58644 (deserialization RCE) added to the chain per yesterday's Microsoft update. Sources: [CISA hardening advisory](https://www.cisa.gov/news-events/alerts/2026/07/14/cisa-urges-sharepoint-hardening-after-new-exploitations), [SecurityWeek](https://www.securityweek.com/cisa-urges-immediate-patching-of-exploited-sharepoint-vulnerabilities/), [Orca Security analysis](https://orca.security/resources/blog/microsoft-july-2026-patch-tuesday-sharepoint-zero-day/), [CyCognito analysis](https://www.cycognito.com/blog/emerging-threat-cve-2026-56164-sharepoint-server-privilege-escalation-via-missing-authentication/).
- **"LegacyHive" Windows User Profile Service arbitrary-hive-load EoP PoC (Chaotic Eclipse / Nightmare-Eclipse, 15 Jul; continued 16 Jul reporting)** — [HIGH] Public PoC dropped hours after July Patch Tuesday, works on all supported Windows desktop and server SKUs including systems already patched with the July 2026 rollup. Published PoC needs additional user credentials and is limited to `usrclass.dat`; the researcher says the private variant does not require credentials and works beyond that hive. Ongoing dispute between the researcher and Microsoft — this is the second major Chaotic Eclipse pre-patch drop of the year. Practitioner take: EoP into SYSTEM from any interactive foothold; opportunistic ransomware uplift is the risk. Sources: [The Hacker News (15 Jul)](https://thehackernews.com/2026/07/researcher-drops-new-windows-zero-day.html), [The Register / LegacyHive coverage](https://www.theregister.com/security/2026/07/15/microsofts-serial-tormentor-drops-legacyhive-0-day/5271723).
- **Sophos State of Ransomware 2026 — identity-based intrusion overtakes exploitation as the leading ransomware root cause** — [NOTABLE, sector data] Survey of 2,158 orgs across 17 countries. Malicious email 26% + phishing 24% > vulnerability exploitation 18% (down from 32% the prior year). 79% of attacks start with compromised identities; 97% of those victims had MFA in place — the takeaway is that partial MFA coverage plus bypass techniques (MFA fatigue, adversary-in-the-middle proxies, session-token theft) are now the operative gap. 67% of victims say the ransomware event was also their most significant identity attack of the year. Aligns with the Scattered Spider prosecution timing above. Sources: [Dark Reading](https://www.darkreading.com/identity-access-management-security/identity-attacks-overtake-exploits-top-ransomware-cause), [Sophos press release (15 Jul)](https://www.sophos.com/en-us/press/press-releases/2026/07/79-percent-ransomware-attacks-originate-from-compromised-identities), [Manila Times syndication](https://www.manilatimes.net/2026/07/15/tmt-newswire/globenewswire/79-of-ransomware-attacks-now-originate-from-compromised-identities-sophos-report-finds/2384845).
