## Telecom

- **Taiwan announces first mobile-internet-disruption civil-defence drill.** Government will throttle 4G/5G in 14 northern/central counties to ~1% of normal capacity on 10 Aug (central) and 13 Aug (northern); voice, SMS, emergency alerts remain up. For a telco offensive-security practitioner this is an unusual test of graceful degradation and QoS/PLMN behaviour under enforced starvation — worth watching what carriers publish about how throttling is enforced (policy-and-charging vs. RAN-side scheduling) and whether any signalling anomalies surface. [Focus Taiwan](https://focustaiwan.tw/society/202607210012), [The Record](https://therecord.media/taiwan-mobile-5g-speed-reductions-han-kuang). Date: 2026-07-21. [NOTABLE]

## General high-severity

- **CISA KEV: four new entries (21 Jul 2026).**
  - **CVE-2026-0770 — Langflow pre-auth RCE (CVSS 9.8).** `validate_code()` passes user-controlled Python into `exec()` with no sandbox; unauthenticated request to `/api/v1/validate/code` yields code execution as the service account. Multiple public PoCs; Sysdig has separately reported an AI-agent-driven operator ("JADEPUFFER") deploying ransomware against Langflow servers. [The Hacker News](https://thehackernews.com/2026/07/cisa-adds-exploited-sharepoint-rce-zero.html), [CVE-2026-0770 PoC](https://github.com/affix/CVE-2026-0770-PoC). Date added: 2026-07-21. [CRITICAL]
  - **CVE-2026-63030 — WordPress Core "Interpretation Conflict" (wp2shell).** Logic flaw in `/wp-json/batch/v1`: a malformed sub-request causes downstream requests to route to unintended handlers, bypassing endpoint allow-lists. Chained with CVE-2026-60137 → unauth RCE. Affects 6.9.0–6.9.4, 7.0.0–7.0.1. Fixed in 6.9.5 / 7.0.2. [SecurityWeek](https://www.securityweek.com/wp2shell-wordpress-vulnerabilities-exploited-in-the-wild/), [VulnCheck writeup](https://www.vulncheck.com/blog/wp2shell), [BleepingComputer](https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/critical-wp2shell-wordpress-flaws-exploited-to-install-webshells/). Date added: 2026-07-21. [CRITICAL]
  - **CVE-2026-60137 — WordPress Core SQL injection (wp2shell).** SQLi in the `author__not_in` parameter of `WP_Query`; on its own high-severity, weaponised alongside 63030 for the full pre-auth-RCE chain. Same affected/fixed versions. [Wiz](https://www.wiz.io/blog/wp2shell-cve-2026-63030-cve-2026-60137). Date added: 2026-07-21. [HIGH]
  - **CVE-2021-27137 — DD-WRT UPnP stack buffer overflow.** Unsafe `strcpy` in UPnP handling; unauth remote overflow when UPnP is enabled. Being used by the C0XMO Gafgyt-variant botnet to enrol routers into DDoS infrastructure. Old bug, freshly weaponised — worth telling network teams to check any DD-WRT edge gear. [CISA KEV catalogue](https://www.cisa.gov/known-exploited-vulnerabilities-catalog). Date added: 2026-07-21. [HIGH]

- **ServiceNow AI Platform CVE-2026-6875 — pre-auth RCE, active exploitation, second sandbox-escape gadget confirmed.** Code-injection via `GlideRecord.addQuery()` `javascript:` prefix, escaping the script sandbox. Exploitation began ~17 Jul per Defused; a distinct gadget chain that bypasses defences tuned to the public PoC has now been confirmed. Hosted instances were patched by ServiceNow in April/June; self-hosted customers must be on Brazil EA/GA, Australia Patch 2, Zurich Patch 7b/9, or Yokohama Patch 12 HF1b/Patch 13. [Help Net Security](https://www.helpnetsecurity.com/2026/07/20/servicenow-cve-2026-6875-exploited/), [SecurityAffairs](https://securityaffairs.com/195723/ai/attackers-exploit-critical-servicenow-rce-flaw-cve-2026-6875.html). Date: 2026-07-20/21. [CRITICAL]

- **Qilin ransomware chaining Palo Alto GlobalProtect CVE-2026-0257 for initial access.** Arctic Wolf's "Cookie Crumbles" report describes multiple June intrusions where an auth-bypass in GlobalProtect (unauthenticated attackers can establish VPN sessions when auth-override cookies are enabled with certain cert configs) was used as the on-ramp for LSASS dumping, NTDS extraction, PsExec lateral movement, Rclone/MEGA exfil, and Qilin encryption. Fix has been available since 13 May; unpatched appliances are being picked off. Coverage broke widely on 21 Jul. [Arctic Wolf](https://arcticwolf.com/resources/blog/exploitation-of-cve-2026-0257-leads-to-qilin-ransomware/), [BleepingComputer](https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/critical-globalprotect-vpn-bug-now-exploited-in-ransomware-attacks/), [SC Media](https://www.scworld.com/news/qilin-exploits-palo-alto-networks-globalprotect-vpn-firewalls). Date: 2026-07-20/21. [HIGH]

- **Atlassian security bulletin (21 Jul 2026).** Coordinated release with 83 high-severity fixes and 18 critical-severity third-party fixes across the product line. Direct bulletin page returned 403 to automated fetches — practitioners should pull the bulletin directly through a browser. Date: 2026-07-21. [HIGH]

## Worth watching

- **ServiceNow bypass-of-the-bypass.** With a second sandbox-escape gadget already public, expect PoC iteration this week — WAF rules matching the first PoC are not sufficient. If you run ServiceNow, prioritise the vendor patch over a virtual-patching stopgap.
- **JADEPUFFER / ENCFORGE against Langflow.** Sysdig has been tracking what looks like AI-agent-driven post-exploitation against exposed AI infra; ransomware compiled in Go targeting AI-workload files. Small footprint today, but the pattern (compromise AI service → deploy AI-aware ransomware) is one to watch for tabletop scenarios.
- **Salt Typhoon "still very much ongoing" (FBI).** No new named-target disclosures in the last 24h, but the FBI's CyberTalks 2026 remarks landed this week — telco IR teams should re-check hunt coverage for the LotL patterns published by CISA/FBI in earlier joint advisories.
- **DD-WRT UPnP re-weaponisation.** The C0XMO botnet using a 2021 bug tells you two things: (1) there's still a pool of exposed DD-WRT with UPnP enabled at the WAN, and (2) low-effort attackers are trawling the CVE backlog for anything mobile-carrier or SMB routers still ship with. Consider a sweep of your own edge fleet for legacy DD-WRT and any UPnP left listening beyond internal interfaces.
