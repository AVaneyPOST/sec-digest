## TL;DR

- **US State Department posts $10M Rewards-for-Justice bounty on UNC5792 +
  UNC4221 (Mon 30 Jun).** The two Russian-intelligence clusters — FSB / FSB
  Border Guards for UNC5792, GRU for UNC4221 — are the ones behind the
  Signal-and-WhatsApp phishing / account-takeover campaign the FBI first
  warned about in March and updated on 26 Jun (PSA I-062626-PSA) to add the
  "hand over your Signal Backup Recovery Key" step. The new tradecraft
  matters to any operator whose engineering, NOC, SOC or exec channels have
  standardised on Signal / WhatsApp for out-of-band comms: a compromised
  recovery key restores the full account backup and message history, past
  and present. [NOTABLE — mobile messaging / geopolitical]
- **Nissan discloses employee data breach linked to Oracle PeopleSoft
  CVE-2026-35273 (Mon 30 Jun).** Nissan warns current and former employees in
  the US, Canada, Mexico and Brazil that payroll records, banking and
  tax data, and Social Security numbers may have been stolen. The
  vulnerability is the same PeopleSoft PeopleTools pre-auth RCE (CVSS 9.8)
  that ShinyHunters weaponised as a zero-day between 27 May – 9 Jun,
  Oracle published a security alert 10 Jun, and CISA added to KEV 12 Jun
  with a 15 Jun federal deadline. Mandiant's tally is 100+ victims, mostly
  in education. [HIGH — Oracle PeopleSoft / HRIS supply chain]
- **Aflac Life Insurance Japan discloses 4.38M-customer breach (Mon 30 Jun).**
  Attackers hit the customer portal between 15 Jun and 25 Jun, the
  operations-team caught it via a traffic-surge anomaly, disclosure landed
  30 Jun. Names, addresses and phone numbers for all 4.38M; premium-payment
  account data for ~230k. US operations unaffected. Attribution not
  disclosed. [HIGH — insurance / APAC]
- **Kaspersky Securelist publishes "The Gentlemen RaaS" write-up (Mon 30
  Jun).** The RaaS group already ranked in H1 2026's top 10 by DLS victim
  count; the new report catalogues an added custom Go backdoor for RCE and
  SOCKS proxying, BYOVD-based EDR-killer (GentleKiller), DLL sideloading,
  PsExec-based lateral movement and a Go-based encryptor obfuscator that
  renames symbols, source files and structs. Same TTPs are already in use
  against critical infrastructure. [NOTABLE — ransomware TTP evolution]
- **No new GSMA / SS7 / Diameter / GTP / N32 / SEPP advisory verified in

## Telecom

- **US State Department Rewards-for-Justice bounty on UNC5792 + UNC4221
  (Mon 30 Jun)** *(NOTABLE — mobile messaging / geopolitical)*
  Up to $10M for information identifying members of two Russian-state
  clusters — UNC5792 (linked to the FSB and its Border Guards element) and
  UNC4221 (linked to Russian military intelligence, i.e. the GRU). The
  campaign these designations cover is not an attack on Signal or WhatsApp
  the protocols; it targets *individual accounts* of US, allied and
  Ukrainian government / military / journalist / political-figure users.
  The 26 Jun PSA I-062626-PSA update from the FBI added the crucial new
  step: attackers no longer just coerce QR-link approvals for linked
  devices, they also elicit the target's Signal Backup Recovery Key. A
  handed-over recovery key gives the attacker full backup restore rights
  and therefore complete historical + ongoing message read. For carriers,
  the operator-side impact is not the messaging platforms themselves but
  the standard telco practice of using Signal / WhatsApp for out-of-band
  ops-channel comms during incidents, change windows and BSS-vendor
  handoffs; those channels are now credibly hostile. [The Record
  <https://therecord.media/10million-reward-us-russian-hackers-unc4221-unc5792>
  / BleepingComputer
  <https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/us-offers-10-million-for-hackers-targeting-whatsapp-signal-users/>
  / SecurityAffairs

## General high-severity

- **Nissan discloses breach of current + former employees via Oracle
  PeopleSoft CVE-2026-35273 zero-day (Mon 30 Jun)** *(CVSS 9.8 pre-auth
  RCE in PeopleSoft PeopleTools, HIGH — HRIS supply chain)*
  Nissan notified current and former employees across the US, Canada,
  Mexico and Brazil that a PeopleSoft-hosted personnel data set has been
  accessed. The company's letter warns SSNs, banking, and financial /
  tax data may have been exfiltrated. The attack chain is the same one
  ShinyHunters ran as a zero-day between 27 May and 9 Jun: pre-auth RCE
  over HTTP to the PeopleSoft PeopleTools servlet, no user interaction,
  server takeover. Oracle published an out-of-band security alert
  10 Jun, CISA added CVE-2026-35273 to KEV 12 Jun with a 15 Jun
  federal-agency deadline. Mandiant estimates 100+ victims, primarily
  in education (Nottingham University, ~450k records, was disclosed
  earlier the same week). For a telco practitioner the transferable
  lesson is that the *HRIS tier* is once again a viable initial-access
  path: PeopleSoft, Workday-integration servlets and their exposed
  admin surfaces sit inside a lot of operator "corporate" VRFs
  reachable from misconfigured jumphosts and shared-BSS environments.
